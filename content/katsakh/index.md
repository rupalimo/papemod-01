Katsakh | քացախ is the Armenian word for vinegar.

The “Katsakh: Mediterranean Archives” project is a personal collection of non-fiction films shot on Super 8mm and 16mm cameras in towns and villages across the eastern Mediterranean from the 1920s to the 1970s. This collection aims to capture the region’s diverse life and culture, serving as a rich historical archive that invites viewers to connect with our past.

Each film in “Katsakh” represents a fight for memory, preserving moments that might otherwise fade. I hope these films spark new stories and conversations, creating a dialogue between the past and present. They serve as a form of resistance, celebrating life in a world that often erases us. They remind us that memory is crucial to our identity and our journey toward liberation.

For inquiries or further information, please contact us via email at katsakh.project@gmail.com
